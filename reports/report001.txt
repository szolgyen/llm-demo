A. Placenta, singleton third-trimester delivery. Received fresh is a singleton placenta weighing 485 g (trimmed), measuring 18.5 × 17.0 × 2.5 cm. The fetal surface is smooth with normally distributed vessels. The membranes are tan-pink, translucent, and complete, with marginal rupture. The umbilical cord measures 42.0 cm in length and 1.4 cm in diameter, with three vessels and eccentric insertion. The maternal surface is complete with intact cotyledons. Sectioning reveals dark red, spongy parenchyma without significant infarction or mass lesions. Sections of the extraplacental membranes demonstrate an acute neutrophilic infiltrate involving the chorion and amnion, consistent with acute chorioamnionitis. The inflammatory infiltrate is moderate in severity and extends through the full thickness of the amnion. The chorionic plate also shows acute inflammation. The umbilical cord demonstrates focal acute funisitis with neutrophilic infiltration of the umbilical vessel walls and Wharton's jelly, consistent with a fetal inflammatory response. Placental villous maturation is appropriate for gestational age. No significant villitis, intervillositis, decidual vasculopathy, or increased perivillous fibrin deposition is identified. No significant infarction or thrombotic lesions are present. Fetal vessels are patent without evidence of fetal vascular malperfusion.
