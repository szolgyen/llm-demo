A. Placenta, singleton stillbirth. Received in formalin is a singleton placenta weighing 320 g (trimmed). The placental disc measures 15.2 × 14.5 × 2.0 cm. The fetal surface is smooth with mildly congested fetal vessels. The membranes are complete and tan-gray with marginal rupture. The umbilical cord measures 28.5 cm in length and 1.2 cm in diameter, with three vessels and a marginal insertion. A true knot is identified approximately 8 cm from the fetal insertion. The maternal surface is complete with intact cotyledons. Serial sectioning demonstrates approximately 20% pale, firm, wedge-shaped infarcts involving the placental parenchyma. No retroplacental hematoma or focal mass lesion is identified. Sections demonstrate multiple remote and recent placental infarcts involving approximately 20% of the placental parenchyma. Decidual arteries show features of maternal vascular malperfusion, including fibrinoid necrosis and acute atherosis. Distal villous hypoplasia and increased syncytial knot formation are present. Intervillous fibrin deposition is mildly increased. The fetal membranes show no significant acute inflammatory infiltrate. The chorionic plate and umbilical cord are without evidence of acute inflammation. Umbilical vessels are patent without thrombosis. No villitis, intervillositis, or fetal vascular malperfusion is identified.
